## Definitions


**1(1)** In this Act:

"award" means an award rendered by the Arbitral Tribunal, established by Article 37 of the Convention, including an interpretation, revision or annulment of the award under the Convention;

"Convention" means the Convention on the Settlement of Investment Disputes between States and Nationals of other States set out in the Schedule.

  (2) Unless the context otherwise requires, words and expressions used in this Act have the same meaning as in the Convention.


## Act prevails


**2** If there is conflict between this Act and any other enactment, this Act prevails.


## Application of Act


**3** This Act applies in respect of

  - (a) agreements recording consent to arbitration or conciliation proceedings entered into under the Convention, and

  - (b) awards rendered,

  including agreements entered into or awards rendered before the coming into force of this Act.


## Application of Convention


**4** The Convention applies in British Columbia.


## Enforceability of awards


**5(1)** A certified copy of an award may be filed with the Supreme Court.

  (2) An award filed under subsection (1) has the same effect and is enforceable as a judgment of the Supreme Court.


## Award final and binding


**6** An award is final and binding and is not subject to appeal, review, setting aside or any other remedy except as provided in the Convention.


## Stay of enforcement proceedings


**7** If enforcement of an award is stayed under the Convention, on the application of a party to arbitration proceedings, the Supreme Court must stay proceedings in relation to enforcement of the award.


## Interim measures


**8** Unless the parties have stipulated to the contrary in their agreement recording their consent to arbitration proceedings under the Convention, a party may not request a court or an administrative tribunal to order interim measures, before the institution of the proceedings or during the proceedings, for the preservation of the party's rights and interests.


## Privilege in relation to conciliation proceedings


**9** Except as the parties otherwise agree, neither party to conciliation proceedings under the Convention is entitled in any other proceedings, whether before a court, an administrative tribunal or arbitrators, or otherwise, to use

  - (a) any views expressed or statements, admissions or offers of settlement made by the other party in the conciliation proceedings, or

  - (b) the report or any recommendations made by the Conciliation Commission.


## Offence Act


**10** Section 5 of the Offence Act does not apply to this Act or the regulations.


## Power to make regulations


**11(1)** The Lieutenant Governor in Council may make regulations referred to in section 41 of the Interpretation Act.

  (2) Without limiting subsection (1), the Lieutenant Governor in Council may make regulations as follows:

  - (a) prescribing the terms and conditions under which the government may enter into an agreement recording consent to arbitration proceedings under the Convention;

  - (b) exempting from an enactment, a person or class of persons who acts in a professional capacity in an arbitration or conciliation proceeding and is not entitled under the enactment to practise that profession in British Columbia;

  - (c) imposing different conditions on exemptions granted under paragraph (b) to different persons or classes of persons.
